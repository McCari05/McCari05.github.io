ε (molar absorptivity) is fixed at 1000 M⁻¹cm⁻¹ and E_c (critical exposure) is fixed at 10 mJ/cm² — both are typical values for common acrylate photoinitiator systems. D_p = 1/(ε·[A]) is the depth at which light attenuates to 1/e (~37%) of I₀. The O₂ model uses C_d = (D_p/n)·ln(I₀ⁿ·t/E_c), where n ∈ (0.68, 1) accounts for radical scavenging by dissolved oxygen. Sliding to "None" sets n = 1, recovering standard Jacob's equation.
